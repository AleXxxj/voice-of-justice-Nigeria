üåç EARLY LIFE & EDUCATION

Prince Abisoye Emmanuel was born on December 25, 1978, in Benin, Edo State. He attended Western Boys Secondary School, graduating in 1997. He proceeded to the University of Benin where he earned a degree in Public Administration in 2002. Growing up, he experienced firsthand the struggles of ordinary Nigerians, which would later shape his advocacy work.

üöó BUSINESS JOURNEY

Starting from humble beginnings, Prince Abisoye Emmanuel built his life through the auto trade industry. He went on to establish multiple auto dealership companies, expanding into importation and exportation. Despite his business success, he never forgot his roots and has consistently used his resources to support community members through philanthropy.

‚öñÔ∏è THE ACTIVISM JOURNEY

Even before public recognition, Prince Abisoye Emmanuel was known for confronting unfair treatment within his environment. Using whatever influence he had, he intervened in disputes, helping victims seek justice. His activism became public in 2024 during Nigeria's intense economic hardship. Seeing the suffering of everyday Nigerians, he began speaking fearlessly against injustice, corruption, and systemic oppression.

The turning point came when his advocacy videos went viral - one reaching 470,000 views on Facebook and 174,000 on TikTok. This platform gave voice to victims like Emmanuel Abarowe (PA to Karowi Bala) and Destiny Peresoudei (brother of Patrick), who came forward with their testimonies after seeing his work.

üí≠ ADVOCACY PHILOSOPHY

Prince Abisoye Emmanuel does not believe in lone heroism. His philosophy is simple but powerful:

"Every Nigerian is an activist. Every Nigerian has a voice."

His mission is to awaken civic courage among citizens and remind them that change is possible when people unite.

üåç VISION FOR NIGERIA

He envisions Nigeria becoming the true Giant of Africa - not just in name, but in justice, unity, and global influence. He believes Nigerians must unite beyond tribe and language to reshape the nation's future and restore its dignity before the world.

"Nigeria is a great and powerful country destined to influence the world positively, but our bad and ignorant leaders won't let that happen. It's on the citizens to either unite together and reshape the story and future of Nigeria and Africa as a whole."

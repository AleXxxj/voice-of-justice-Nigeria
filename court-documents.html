THE 17 RELIEFS SOUGHT

1

DECLARATION

That the 1st to 11th Respondents, not being law enforcement authorities, have no right to arrest, detain, torture or maltreat the Applicants.

2

DECLARATION

That the Applicants' Right to Life under Section 33 includes protection from unlawful arrest and detention.

3

DECLARATION

That the arrest and prolonged detention of the Applicants is unlawful, illegal and unconstitutional.

4

DECLARATION

That the torture and degrading treatment violates Section 34 (Right to Dignity of Human Person).

5

DECLARATION

That the arrest and detention violates Section 35 (Right to Personal Liberty).

6

DECLARATION

That the arrest and detention violates Section 41 (Right to Freedom of Movement).

7

DECLARATION

That the operation of a private/personal prison by the 1st and 2nd respondents is unconstitutional and illegal.

8

ORDER

Mandating release of all persons currently incarcerated in the Oporoza prison.

9

ORDER

Directing release of CHARLES KORUBO, being held in Oporoza.

10

ORDER

Directing release of CHIEF PRIEST KAROWEI BALA, being held in Oporoza.

11

ORDER

Directing 12th and 13th Respondents to stop releasing funds to 1st and 2nd Respondents under any pipeline surveillance project.

12

ORDER

Mandating Police and DSS to investigate the activities of 1st-11th Respondents regarding Oporoza private prison.

13

INJUNCTION

Restraining 1st-11th Respondents from further arresting, detaining, torturing the Applicants.

14

INJUNCTION

Restraining 12th and 13th Respondents from further releasing funds to 1st and 2nd Respondents.

15

ORDER

Compelling 1st-11th Respondents to tender a formal written apology to the Applicants and publish in three national dailies.

16
